Donkey Kong

Donkey Kong is a classic arcade game released by Nintendo in 1981. It launched not only the Donkey Kong series but also introduced the world to a future legend — Mario (then known as Jumpman).

The game was created by game designer Shigeru Miyamoto. The plot is simple: a giant gorilla, Donkey Kong, kidnaps a woman named Pauline and climbs a construction site. The player controls a carpenter named Jumpman (the future Mario), who must climb to the top by jumping over barrels and avoiding fireballs.

Donkey Kong became one of the most successful arcade games of the 1980s and helped Nintendo establish itself in the American market. The game featured four unique stages: barrels, a conveyor with pies, springs, and the rivets stage.

The original arcade cabinet ran on a Z80 processor and played Nintendo's signature music and sound effects. Today, Donkey Kong is rightly considered a classic and the ancestor of the platformer genre.
